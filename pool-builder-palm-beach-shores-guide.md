## Understanding Pool Construction in Coastal Environments

### Unique Considerations for Palm Beach Shores

Building a pool in Palm Beach Shores requires specialized knowledge that goes
beyond standard pool construction. Several factors unique to barrier island
living come into play, and understanding these helps you appreciate why
expertise matters so much.

 **High water table management:** The water table in Palm Beach Shores sits
particularly high due to the barrier island 's proximity to both the Atlantic
Ocean and the Intracoastal Waterway. Groundwater can rise to within a few feet
of the surface, creating hydrostatic pressure that can literally push an empty
pool shell out of the ground—a phenomenon called "floating" that causes
catastrophic damage.

Proper engineering addresses this through several methods: hydrostatic relief
valves that allow groundwater to enter the pool when it's empty, proper
drainage systems around the pool structure, adequate structural weight to
counteract buoyancy forces, and construction timing that accounts for tidal
influences and seasonal water table variations.

 **Salt air and corrosion resistance:** The ocean 's proximity means salt-
laden air constantly contacts pool equipment, fixtures, and structural
elements. Standard materials that work fine inland fail prematurely here. We
specify marine-grade stainless steel for all metal components, equipment with
corrosion-resistant housing designed for coastal environments, premium plaster
or aggregate finishes that resist salt exposure, and protective coatings on
all exposed metal surfaces.

 **Proper drainage systems:** Heavy rainfall, high water tables, and potential
storm surge all require carefully engineered drainage. Poor drainage leads to
deck flooding, soil erosion, and structural problems. We design comprehensive
drainage that moves water away from your pool and home efficiently.

 **Wind load considerations for screen enclosures:** If you want a screen
enclosure (popular for keeping out leaves and insects), coastal wind loads are
significantly higher than inland. Engineering must account for hurricane-force
winds, and construction must meet stringent building codes specific to coastal
exposure zones.

 **Compliance with coastal construction codes:** Palm Beach Shores and Palm
Beach County have specific requirements for coastal properties, including
setbacks from dunes and shorelines, environmental protection measures, flood
zone compliance, and structural standards that exceed inland requirements.

### Pool Types Perfect for Waterfront Properties

 **Concrete/Gunite Pools:** The premium choice for [Palm Beach
Shores](https://www.palmbeachshoresfl.us/) properties, concrete pools offer
unlimited design flexibility and superior durability against coastal
conditions. We can create any shape you can imagine, incorporate infinity
edges that mirror ocean views and create visual flow with the horizon,
integrate elaborate water features from naturalistic rock formations to modern
sheet descents, and ensure structural integrity that lasts decades even in
challenging coastal conditions.

The construction process creates a monolithic structure—one solid piece of
reinforced concrete without seams or joints that could fail. This makes
concrete pools incredibly durable and perfect for the long term.

 **Fiberglass Pools:** These manufactured shells provide several advantages
for coastal properties: excellent resistance to osmotic pressure from high
water tables due to their non-porous gelcoat surface, faster installation
timelines (ideal for seasonal residents who want minimal disruption), smooth
surfaces that resist algae growth and require fewer chemicals, and lower long-
term maintenance requirements compared to concrete.

Fiberglass pools work beautifully in Palm Beach Shores, though design options
are limited to available shell models. If you find a shape you love,
fiberglass delivers quality and longevity with less maintenance.

 **Infinity Edge and Vanishing Edge Designs:** Particularly stunning in Palm
Beach Shores where water views are spectacular, these designs create visual
continuity with ocean or Intracoastal views. The pool appears to merge
seamlessly with the horizon, creating a dramatic effect that never gets old.

Infinity edges require precise engineering—the catch basin, sophisticated
plumbing, and exact leveling all must be perfect. But the results are
absolutely breathtaking, especially for properties with elevated views or
direct sightlines to the water.

 **2\. Can I build a pool on a waterfront property in Palm Beach Shores?**

Absolutely! Many of our most stunning projects are on waterfront lots with

engineering to address high water tables (often within 1-2 feet of ground
surface on waterfront lots), proper drainage systems that protect both your
property and coastal water quality, setback requirements from bulkheads or
seawalls (typically 7.5-10 feet depending on specific regulations), and wind
load considerations for any structures like screen enclosures. Our experience
with waterfront construction means we know how to navigate these challenges
efficiently. We work with coastal engineers who understand barrier island
construction, coordinate with environmental agencies when required, and ensure
your pool is properly designed to last for decades despite the challenging
coastal environment. The results are worth it—imagine an infinity edge that
seems to merge with the ocean beyond, or a beautiful pool framed by palms with
Intracoastal views creating a true paradise.

 **3\. What type of pool is best for a coastal property?**

Concrete/gunite pools are ideal for Palm Beach Shores because they can be
engineered specifically to handle high water table conditions through proper
structural design and hydrostatic relief systems, offer complete design
flexibility to complement your views and property (including infinity edges,
custom shapes, and integrated features), and accept premium finishes that
resist salt air corrosion and maintain their beauty in coastal environments.
The construction creates a monolithic structure—one solid piece of reinforced
concrete—that's incredibly durable. Fiberglass pools are also excellent
choices and offer faster installation timelines (typically 4-6 weeks versus
10-14 weeks for concrete), inherent resistance to osmotic pressure from
groundwater, smooth surfaces that resist algae and require less chemical
maintenance, and lower long-term maintenance overall. The best choice depends
on your priorities: concrete for maximum customization and architectural
integration, or fiberglass for faster installation and easier maintenance. For
properties with spectacular views, concrete's ability to create infinity edges
makes it the preferred choice for high-end installations.
