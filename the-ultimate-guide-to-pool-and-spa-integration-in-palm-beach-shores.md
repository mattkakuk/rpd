## Design Styles for Integrated Pool & Spa Systems

### Spillover Spas – The Most Popular Choice

Spillover spas sit slightly higher than the pool, allowing warm spa water to
cascade into the pool. They offer:

  * Visual elegance

  * Gentle waterfall sound

  * Improved circulation and aeration

  * Natural temperature moderation

Styles range from modern knife-edge spillways to natural stacked-stone
waterfalls.

### Vanishing Edge (Infinity) Spas

Perfect for waterfront and modern homes, vanishing-edge spas create the
illusion of endless water flowing into the horizon. These designs:

  * Maximize water views

  * Pair beautifully with contemporary architecture

  * Create dramatic, resort-level aesthetics

They require precise engineering but deliver breathtaking results.

### Perimeter Overflow Spas

These mirror-like designs maintain perfectly level water surfaces on both pool
and spa. Water continuously flows over all edges into a hidden gutter system,
creating:

  * Reflective, glass-smooth surfaces

  * Resort-quality luxury

  * Exceptional sunrise and sunset views

They require higher technical precision and investment but are visually
unmatched.

### Raised Bond Beam Spas

Raised spas are elevated significantly above the pool, creating bold multi-
level designs. They:

  * Provide dramatic waterfalls

  * Improve accessibility

  * Allow for enhanced lighting effects

  * Create stunning landscaping opportunities

These spas often become the visual centerpiece of the backyard.

## Technical Considerations

### Shared vs Independent Systems

Most integrated designs use **shared filtration** with **separate heating
controls** , which provides:

  * Lower equipment cost

  * Easier maintenance

  * Individual spa temperature control

Independent systems are used only in large or specialized projects.

### Water Chemistry Management

Because pool and spa water mix, maintaining chemistry is simpler than managing
two separate systems. [RPD Custom
pools](https://maps.app.goo.gl/VmvSdh5PC7BXqaHS6) strongly recommends
**saltwater chlorination** , which provides:

  * Automatic sanitation

  * Softer water feel

  * Less chemical odor

  * Lower long-term maintenance

Automated pH control systems further improve water balance and equipment
lifespan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1\. Can I add an integrated spa to my existing pool in Palm Beach Shores?

Yes. In most cases, an integrated spa can be added to an existing pool through
structural tie-ins and shared plumbing connections. A professional site
inspection is required to evaluate pool structure, plumbing access, and
equipment capacity before installation.

### 2\. How long does it take to build an integrated pool and spa system?

Typical construction timelines range from **6 to 10 weeks** , depending on
design complexity, permitting, weather conditions, and material availability.
More advanced designs like vanishing edges or perimeter overflow systems may
require additional time.

### 3\. Do integrated spas increase electricity or gas bills significantly?

Not significantly when designed correctly. Modern variable-speed pumps,
insulated spa covers, and high-efficiency heaters keep operating costs low.
Many homeowners find their spa adds only **$50–$120 per month** depending on
usage.

### 4\. What size spa is best for a residential backyard?

The most popular residential spa sizes comfortably seat **4 to 6 people** ,
balancing comfort, energy efficiency, and space. Larger spas are available for
homeowners who frequently entertain or want multiple therapy zones.

### 5\. Are integrated pool and spa systems safe for children and seniors?

Yes. Integrated spas can include non-slip steps, handrails, bench seating,
depth controls, and temperature limits to ensure safety for all ages. Many
Palm Beach Shores homeowners request these features for family-friendly
designs.
